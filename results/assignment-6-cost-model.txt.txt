Date: 2024-04-06
Region: us-east-1

=== Traffic Model ===

Peak: 100 RPS × 30 min/day = 180,000 requests/day
Normal: 5 RPS × 5.5 hours/day = 99,000 requests/day
Idle: 18 hours/day (0 RPS)
Total: 279,000 requests/day = 8,370,000 requests/month

=== Monthly Cost Calculation ===

LAMBDA (512 MB, ~207ms avg duration):
- Request cost: 8,370,000 / 1,000,000 × $0.20 = $1.67
- GB-seconds: 8,370,000 × 0.207 sec × 0.5 GB = 866,295 GB-sec
- Duration cost: 866,295 × $0.0000166667 = $14.44
- TOTAL: $16.11/month

FARGATE (0.5 vCPU, 1 GB, 24/7):
- $0.024685/hour × 24 × 30 = $17.77/month

EC2 (t3.small, 24/7):
- $0.0208/hour × 24 × 30 = $14.98/month

=== Break-Even Analysis ===

Lambda cost per request:
- Request fee: $0.20 / 1,000,000 = $0.0000002
- Duration fee: 0.207 sec × 0.5 GB × $0.0000166667 = $0.000001725
- Total per request: $0.000001925

Break-even vs Fargate:
- Fargate monthly: $17.77
- Break-even requests: $17.77 / $0.000001925 = 9,231,169 requests/month
- Break-even RPS: 9,231,169 / (30 × 24 × 3600) = 3.56 RPS

Break-even vs EC2:
- EC2 monthly: $14.98
- Break-even requests: $14.98 / $0.000001925 = 7,781,818 requests/month
- Break-even RPS: 7,781,818 / (30 × 24 × 3600) = 3.00 RPS

=== RECOMMENDATION ===

For the given traffic model (sporadic, spiky, p99 < 500ms SLO):

RECOMMENDED: Lambda with Provisioned Concurrency

Justification:
1. COST: Lambda ($16.11) is comparable to EC2 ($14.98) and cheaper than Fargate ($17.77)
2. SCALING: Lambda auto-scales instantly, no capacity planning needed
3. IDLE COST: Lambda has zero cost during 18h idle period (vs $14.98 EC2, $17.77 Fargate)

CHANGES NEEDED TO MEET SLO:
- Enable Provisioned Concurrency (10 instances) to eliminate cold starts
- Estimated additional cost: 10 × 0.5 GB × 3600 × 24 × 30 × $0.000004 = ~$51.84/month
- Total with PC: ~$68/month

ALTERNATIVE: EC2 with Auto Scaling
- Baseline: 1 × t3.small ($14.98/month)
- Add instances during peak traffic
- Requires more operational overhead

CONDITIONS FOR REVERSAL:
- If average RPS > 3.56: Consider Fargate/EC2 (Lambda becomes more expensive)
- If SLO relaxed to p99 < 2000ms: Lambda without PC is acceptable
- If traffic becomes predictable: EC2 with reserved instances is cheapest
