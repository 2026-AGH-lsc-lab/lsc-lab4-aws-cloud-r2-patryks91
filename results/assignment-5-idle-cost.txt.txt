=== Assignment 5: Cost at Zero Load ===

Region: us-east-1
Date: 2026-04-06

=== AWS Pricing ===

Lambda (x86):
- Requests: $0.20 per 1M requests
- Duration: $0.0000166667 per GB-second
- Idle cost: $0.00 (pay only for invocations)

Fargate (0.5 vCPU, 1 GB RAM):
- vCPU: $0.04048/hour × 0.5 = $0.02024/hour
- Memory: $0.004445/hour × 1 GB = $0.004445/hour
- Total: $0.024685/hour

EC2 (t3.small: 2 vCPU, 2 GB RAM):
- On-demand: $0.0208/hour

=== Monthly Idle Cost (24/7 running, 0 requests) ===

Lambda:      $0.00
Fargate:     $0.024685 × 24 × 30 = $17.77
EC2:         $0.0208 × 24 × 30 = $14.98

=== Monthly Cost (18h idle, 6h active per day) ===

Lambda:      $0.00 (zero idle cost - pay per request only)
Fargate:     $17.77 (runs 24/7 regardless of traffic)
EC2:         $14.98 (runs 24/7 regardless of traffic)

=== Key Insights ===

1. Lambda has ZERO idle cost - you only pay when requests are processed
2. EC2 is ~16% cheaper than Fargate for always-on workloads ($14.98 vs $17.77)
3. Fargate and EC2 cost the same whether idle or active (always-on)
4. For sporadic/unpredictable traffic, Lambda is most cost-effective at low volumes
5. Break-even analysis needed to determine when Lambda becomes more expensive (Assignment 6)
